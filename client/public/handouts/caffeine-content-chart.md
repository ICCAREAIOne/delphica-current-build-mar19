# Caffeine Content Reference Chart

## Understanding Your Caffeine Intake

Caffeine is a stimulant that can provide temporary energy, but excessive consumption or sudden withdrawal can cause fatigue, headaches, and other symptoms. Use this chart to track your daily caffeine intake and make informed decisions.

---

## Recommended Daily Limits

| Group | Maximum Daily Caffeine |
|-------|------------------------|
| **Healthy Adults** | 400 mg (about 4 cups of coffee) |
| **Pregnant Women** | 200 mg (about 2 cups of coffee) |
| **Adolescents (12-18)** | 100 mg (about 1 cup of coffee) |
| **Children (under 12)** | Not recommended |

**Note**: Individual sensitivity varies. Some people experience side effects at lower doses.

## Signs You May Be Consuming Too Much Caffeine

- Jitteriness or shakiness
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat
- Difficulty sleeping (even when consumed 6+ hours before bed)
- Anxiety or nervousness
- Stomach upset or acid reflux
- Headaches
- Frequent urination
- Muscle tremors
- Need increasing amounts to feel the same effect (tolerance)

---

## Caffeine Withdrawal Symptoms

If you regularly consume caffeine and suddenly stop, you may experience:

- **Headache** (most common)
- **Fatigue and drowsiness**
- **Difficulty concentrating**
- **Irritability and mood changes**
- **Flu-like symptoms**

**Timeline**:
- Symptoms begin: 12-24 hours after last dose
- Peak intensity: 20-51 hours
- Duration: 2-9 days

**To avoid withdrawal**: Taper gradually (see Caffeine Tapering Schedule handout)

---

## Tips for Reducing Caffeine

1. **Track your current intake** using this chart
2. **Set a daily limit** (e.g., 300 mg, then 200 mg)
3. **Reduce gradually** - Cut by 25% each week
4. **Switch to lower-caffeine options**:
   - Regular coffee → Half-caf → Decaf
   - Black tea → Green tea → Herbal tea
   - Energy drinks → Coffee → Tea
5. **Avoid caffeine after 2 PM** to improve sleep
6. **Replace with water** to stay hydrated
7. **Get adequate sleep** (7-9 hours) to reduce need for caffeine

---

## When to See Your Doctor

Contact your healthcare provider if:
- You experience severe withdrawal symptoms
- You're unable to reduce caffeine despite wanting to
- You have heart palpitations or chest pain
- You're pregnant and unsure about safe caffeine levels
- You're taking medications that may interact with caffeine

---

**Remember**: Moderate caffeine consumption (200-300 mg/day) is generally safe for most adults, but individual tolerance varies. Listen to your body!
